English lets you move the spotlight. Today: three ways to move it.

The defence · Weapon 1 · the spotlight sentence

Liam sent the photo to the class group yesterday.

A flat report. Nobody is in trouble yet. Now watch the spotlight move.

It + was + spotlight + who / that

The spotlight works in defence too

— You told the teacher.

— It wasn't me who told the teacher.

— It was someone else who told her.

You don't deny everything. You move the spotlight off yourself.

The counterpunch · Weapon 2

— You didn't do your homework.

— I did do my homework.

— You're not listening to me.

— I am listening!

How it works

Is there already an auxiliary? Stress it.
am · is · have · will · can

No auxiliary? Add do / does / did.

One small word cancels the whole accusation. This is the weapon you'll actually use.

Rapid fire · pairs · A accuses, B counterpunches

01  You don't like my cooking.

02  You weren't at training on Monday.

03  You never answer my messages.

04  You forgot my birthday.

Then swap roles. The stress goes on the auxiliary — say it like you mean it.

The closing speech · Weapon 3

We have never faced a challenge like this.

Never have we faced a challenge like this.

Which one sounds like a prime minister?

You've heard this weapon a thousand times.

Never before has the world seen anything like it.

The trailer voice. Negative word first → subject and auxiliary swap places.
Say it aloud — in the deepest voice you own. That's the register: trailers, speeches, closing arguments. Not the supermarket.

The trial · pairs · 3 minutes

The class thinks you ruined the group project.

The truth: Marek deleted the file — and it happened after you went home.

Build your defence: one cleft + one counterpunch.
Trailer voice = bonus points. Strongest defence gets heard by the court.

One possible defence

It wasn't me who deleted the file.

It was Marek who deleted it — after I went home.

I did do my part of the project.

Never have I deleted someone else's work.

Three weapons, one argument. The last line is the closing speech — save it for the end.

The verdict

The spotlight doesn't change the facts.

It changes what people notice.
